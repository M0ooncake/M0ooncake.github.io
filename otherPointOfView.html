Construct 3

  The idea for this game was simple. I wanted to try and make a platformer, with good feeling movement, and an extremely fluid combat system. I would like to think both of those goals were achieved, though as my first ever attempt into an engine, it was quite ambitious. Over the course of that term, 3 months, it was very rewarding to see it slowly develop into what I hoped it would be.

Pre-Development

  At the start of development it was quite slow. There was a significant portion of time that needed to spent looking online for various royalty free assets. I needed to acquire sounds and sprites. For all sorts of different entities.
  It has been so long since I worked on this, I do not even remember where exactly I found these assets to begin with. However, suffice to say, once various assets were gathered, work could actually begin on development.

Development

  During the beginning it took awhile to understand how engines work. How much work they lift off the developers shoulders and handle behind the scenes. Even then that lead to needing to understand how to work within the bounds of said engine
